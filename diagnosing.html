Recognizing, Diagnosing, and Recovering from Errors


"Error messages should be expressed in plain language (no codes), precisely indicate the problem, and constructively suggest a solution."

Jakob Nielsen

A good website should provide the user with aid when errors occur, clearly explaining what happened, and how to recover from that error.


An error that a user may encounter is receiving “No Results” after entering a search query. Simply stating “No Results” does not help the user broaden their search (instead, they could have suggested typing keywords that were less specific). When a potential spelling mistake is made, the search system will suggest alternatives (for example, if you misspelled “library” by typing “library”, the system will correct you and show search results for that query).


Another error is when a user stumbles upon a broken link or 404 page. The 404 pages on this website are very good – they offer information on the page where you came from, who you can contact about the problem, and provides three alternative options (home, search, or index).
