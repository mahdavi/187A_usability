Error Prevention


"Even better than good error messages is a careful design which prevents a problem from occurring in the first place. Either eliminate error-prone conditions or check for them and present users with a confirmation option before they commit to the action."

Jakob Nielsen

Error prevention is what allows the site to catch errors before they occur. Examples of this is notifying users that when clicking on link that will take you to another page, the site will warn you that you will be leaving the page. Having a good search box also provides good error prevention.


For this site, the search page is pretty straight forward and seems to find everything available. Also, they have a legacy search page which allows you to "match" "format" and "sort" which is very convenient. One major problem that this site does have is when you click on an external link, it doesnâ€™t notify you that you are leaving the page. For library, research, and u-link, they navigate you to another site with warning you first. In addition to this, after you leave the site, there is no clear way for you to return back. Any form of pop-up can resolve this problem.


When you type in the wrong URL, the site does provide an error message telling you to re-check your spelling and offers a contact email for you to refer your problem to and three pages that may help you, like the home page, the search page, and the site map page. If you came from a different page to the 404 page, they also provide a referring page listing where you came from and what other links are there that you could possibly be looking for. This is effective and helpful.
