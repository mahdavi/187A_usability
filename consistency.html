Consistency and Standards


"Users should not have to wonder whether different words, situations, or actions mean the same thing. Follow platform conventions."

Jakob Nielsen

Consistency refers to the page-layouts for each page staying the same, using same terms, and just overall giving the users a feeling of familiarity and uniformity.


For this site, the links library, research, and ulink have completely different page layouts than the other pages. They are different pages but nothing about them relates to the Louisiana.edu website. The nav bar is different and the color schemes are a bit different as well.


In student life there is a tab called map but in the “about us” section its called the campus map. In research, clicking on the logo does not take you to the university website whereas the logo does for the other pages. On the research page, it also uses "University website" instead of "home" to take you back to the Louisiana.edu site. The home page itself also has a completely different layout than the other pages. The search, index, contact us, and ulink are at the bottom of the home page but for every other page its on the top. For the "news" page, the banner says communications and marketing, not news, making it very confusing. Also, within the new page, some of subtabs on top say "new and events" while others just say "news".
