Aesthetic and Minimalist Design


"Dialogues should not contain information which is irrelevant or rarely needed. Every extra unit of information in a dialogue competes with the relevant units of information and diminishes their relative visibility."

Jakob Nielsen

Websites should not be cluttered and information should not be presented to the user all at once. Unnecessary information on a page only acts to distract users from the important information, therefore, it is important to have good aesthetics when organizing a site.


The home page is clean, but when you truly explore, the site becomes very messy. The color scheme of the site, however, is consistent throughout. But there are certain pages that are cluttered with too many words and link, and have no real organization.


The “about us” page has no categorization.Academics does, but there are too many subcategories, and that a drop-down menu would have been a better alternative. For the news section, unlike the other sections, you can not click on the subcategories. Aside from the images that rotate on refresh, the site is very static.
