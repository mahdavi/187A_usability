Closing

By using Nielsen’s ten heuristics, we have covered just a minor portion of problems we found in Louisiana’s university website. Our main concern was the lack of organization and difficult navigation through the site. With no active links and any form of breadcrumbs or directory, it was very easy to get lost and backtrack when we were browsing around. We can see new users and even frequent users becoming extremely frustrated in just a short amount of time. As far as layout and aesthetics, we had no problems with a majority of it.


A lot of the problems with this site can be solved with simple design changes. Functions such as breadcrumbs and active links do not take a lot of time and can be implemented with ease. Simple consistency errors such as non-matching banners are just designing mistakes and can also be fixed with ease.


As far as content, the site provides almost everything that a student, visitor, or staff could possible want. Even though it would probably take them a long time to search for it, the existence of a search bar makes this task easier.

Overall, if we could rate this site based on content, design, and functionality, we would give it a 6 out of 10.
