Match Between System and the Real World


"The system should speak the user's language, with words, phrases and concepts familiar to the user, rather than system-oriented terms. Follow real-world conventions, making information appear in a natural and logical order."

Jakob Nielsen

This means that labels, metaphors, and abbreviations should be easy to decipher by the users. Common terms should be used and if the term needs to be explained, they probably should not have been used. For this site, most of the terms used are normal terms and speaks to both students and adults. There are some that need to be explained though.


Under “about us,” they use the term SACS-COC Reaffirmation of Accreditation. Even when you click into this link, it still doesn't explain what this is or what the acronym stands for. Also in the SACS-COC page, there is a tab for "QEP" and that term isn't defined until you navigate into that page and read the submenus that define it. Under academics, it says “distance and electronic learning.” Although this is a term that could be understood by those looking for it, it could be confusing for new users. Online learning or learning from home would be better alternatives. Under student life it says Bursar/Student Cashier Center. Bursar’s are only common in America in higher learning education systems so unless the user is a part of that system, the term would be completely new to them.
