Flexibility and Efficiency of Use


"Accelerators -- unseen by the novice user -- may often speed up the interaction for the expert user such that the system can cater to both inexperienced and experienced users. Allow users to tailor frequent actions."

Jakob Nielsen

A website should be flexible, providing efficient browsing for both advanced and novice users. More experienced users should be provided the options to quickly and efficiently maneuver the site, while beginners should be provided the support necessary for their purposes.


For the index, after each letter section, they repeat the alphabet links making it efficient to navigate so you don’t have to keep going back to the top. Other than that, there are no breadcrumbs and no sense of navigation to speed up your navigation around the site. This inconsistency makes it harder to speed up the interaction and navigation. The links, however, highlight when you hover over them so you know they are links.


Only the homepage has links to prospective students, current students, alumni, donors and friends, otherwise it’s impossible to find them unless you search it in the search bar. You have to keep going back to the home page in order to browse such options, and the home button is extremely tiny and not obvious. In addition, there are no "active" tabs so you don't know which page you are on, slowing down users in finding other links.
