User Control and Freedom


"Users often choose system functions by mistake and will need a clearly marked "emergency exit" to leave the unwanted state without having to go through an extended dialogue. Support undo and redo."

Jakob Nielsen

User control and freedom is what allows the users to be comfortable and feel “at home” when they are using a site. If users get lost, or want to backtrack, they should be able to do so without frustration.


For this site, it is extremely easy to get lost. For one, there are no breadcrumbs in the site’s navigation which makes it extremely hard to navigate through. Also, because the site has many different sub-links that take you to different pages and different sections, the lack of breadcrumbs makes navigating around the site even more confusing.


Examples of this are: When you click "library", there is no obvious way of how to get back to the home page. When you click "Research" to go to the research page, it doesn't say “home” in the navigation anymore, it says “University website.” In addition, the home link is also somewhat hidden on each page. The one thing that is good though is that the navigation bar on the the top does stay consistent for the most part. Only when you go to library, research, or u-link does the navigation bar change.
